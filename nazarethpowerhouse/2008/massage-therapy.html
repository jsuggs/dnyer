Massage Therapy

Healers throughout time all over the world have instinctively and independently developed a wide range of therapeutic techniques using touch. Many are still in use today, and with good reason. We now have scientific proof of the benefits of massage - benefits ranging from treatment of chronic diseases and injuries to alleviating the stress from everyday life.
Having a massage does more than just relax your body and mind. There are specific physiological and psychological changes that occur - even more so when massage is used as a preventative. Regular massage therapy is not simply a luxury. It not only feels good, but it can cure what ails you.
Swedish/Relaxation Massage: This massage consists of long, gliding strokes. Helps relax tense, tight, or sore muscles. Also relieves mental tension, leaving you physically and emotionally revitalized.
Sports Massage: This massage is designed to prepare athletes of any level for peak performance. Helps drain away fatigue, relieves swelling, reduces muscle tension, promotes flexibility, and helps prevent injuries.
Deep Tissue Massage: This massage focuses on the deeper layers of muscle tissue. It aims to release chronic patterns of tension throughout the body. Slow strokes and deep finger pressure are used on the contracted areas by following and/or going across the fibers of the muscles, tendons, and fascia.
Hot Stone Massage: Experience the benefits of relaxation and wellbeing with this massage that uses warmed lava rocks. Stones are placed both on and under the body on main energy centers. A deeply relaxing and rewarding experience.
Reiki: This is a safe, gentle, and non-invasive form of hands-on healing. Reiki treatments increase energy, reduce pain, and bring the client into a state of deep relaxation and general feeling of wellbeing.
Reflexology: An ancient Chinese technique, which uses pressure point massage on the feet. This restores the flow of energy through the entire body.
